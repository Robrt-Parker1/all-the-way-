Understanding the basics of pressure washing

Pressure washing involves using high-pressure water spray to remove dirt, mold, grime, mud, and other contaminants from surfaces like buildings, driveways, sidewalks, and decks. A professional pressure washing service utilizes advanced equipment to safely clean various surfaces without causing damage. This is especially important for property owners looking to enhance the visual appeal of their surroundings quickly and efficiently.

How pressure washing equipment works

The core of any pressure washing service is the equipment. These machines use an electric or gas-powered motor to drive a water pump, which accelerates water through a nozzle at high speed. The strength of this water stream is measured in PSI (pounds per square inch) and can vary based on the surface being cleaned. Trained technicians at apressure washing service in Macon GA know how to adjust these settings to avoid damage to delicate areas while ensuring thorough cleaning.

Difference between pressure washing and power washing

Although the terms are often used interchangeably, pressure washing andpower washing are slightly different. Power washing typically involves using heated water, which is especially effective in removing tough stains like grease and oil. Pressure washing, while similar, does not always use hot water but is equally effective on general surfaces. Both methods can restore surfaces to their original state, making them look new again.
